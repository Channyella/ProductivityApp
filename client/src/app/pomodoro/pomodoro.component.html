The Pomodoro technique is a time management method.

It involves focusing for work intervals of 25 minutes, taking a short 5 minute break in-between for four cycles, aka a 'pomodoro'. Afterwards take a longer break of 15-30 minutes. (Our timer is 15 minutes long). This helps you stay focused and refreshed to tackle your tasks throughout the day.


Choose a Task

Select a task or project to work on.


Set a Timer

Click on 'pomodoro'. This will start the 25 minute timer. During this time, focus only on the task you chose. This work period is called a 'pomodoro'.


Work Until the Timer Rings

If something pops up during your work time, jot down a note to take care of it during a break if you can. Try to stay focused on your task for the whole pomodoro.


Take a Short Break

After the timer rings, take a 5 minute break. Use this time to relax, stretch, get water, or do something unrelated to your current task.


Repeat the Cycle

After the break, start another pomodoro. Complete four pomodoros, then take a longer break.


Long Break

After completing your four pomodoro cycles, take a longer break, typically 15 to 30 minutes.

This will help you refresh and get ready for your next pomodoro cycle.


Happy Task Completing!
